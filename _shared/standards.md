# Shared Standards (loaded by every SEO-side agent)

The non-negotiable floor for every page and asset. Imported by the root CLAUDE.md, so it loads in every session.

Precedence: these standards are the floor. `seo/context/brand-guidelines.md` may ADD restrictions (more banned words, regulated claims, competitor exclusions) but never relaxes anything here. `seo/context/tone-of-voice.md` governs voice within these rules. A per-page brief may tighten a numeric target, never loosen a rule.

Enforcement: prompts and CLAUDE.md are guidance, not hard gates. For blog posts, the content-writer's Step 5 self-review is the gate and must pass before a post is considered done; there is no separate lint script. Technical and schema checks are enforced by the `onsite-audit` agent. The Project Track runs the full QA pass below as a one-time pre-launch gate. Run the checks. Do not assume they passed. To block an action mechanically, add a PreToolUse hook.

## Writing
- No em dashes anywhere. Use commas, colons, or periods.
- No AI-tell vocabulary or filler. Banned: "in today's fast-paced digital world", "unlock the power of", "leverage", "game-changing", "revolutionize", "seamlessly", "whether you are a small business or large enterprise", "it is important to note", "delve", "tapestry", "navigate the landscape", "robust", "elevate", "supercharge".
- Plain language. Short sentences where they help. Strong opinions only where justified by proof. Real, specific examples over generic ones.
- One signature term site-wide, set in `project_config.md` and mirrored to `seo/context/site-config.md`. FAQ and process sections at Grade 8 reading level. Hero sections may be more literary but must pass a 5-second clarity test.

## Uniqueness (run a duplicate-string scan before output)
- No sentence of 8 or more words may appear on more than one page.
- Any section label may repeat at most twice across the site.
- Each service page's "fit" or "who this is for" section must lead with a different industry and a different one-line worked example. Shared structure is allowed. Shared wording is not.

## Keywords
- One winnable primary per page: long-tail or qualified (for example "AI consultant for small business", "fractional CMO for ecommerce"). Plus 2 to 3 secondaries.
- Head terms are themes for clustering, never page targets.
- Three Kings placement: the primary keyword appears in the title, in the first paragraph, and in at least two H2s. The content-writer checks this in Step 5; apply it to money pages by hand.

## Titles, headings, structure
- Title tags 60 characters or fewer, including the brand.
- Meta description 150 to 160 characters, one per page, unique across the site, written to earn the click, with the primary keyword present.
- Headings (H1 to H4) name document sections only. Slogans and taglines are styled paragraphs, never headings.
- Decorative glyphs are delivered via CSS, never typed into text nodes. Specify this in the Dev brief.

## Metrics
- Every metric carries a currency unit (where money is involved) and a timeframe.
- No metric contradicts another anywhere on the site.
- No vanity metric appears on a page that argues against vanity metrics.
- Never fabricate a metric. Search volume, keyword difficulty, and CPC come from DataForSEO (primary) or Semrush (failover). Backlink and authority metrics come from Semrush. If a value is unknown, leave it null.

## CTAs and links
- No CTA or internal link may point to a page that does not exist: in the planned sitemap (Project Track) or the live sitemap (Operations Track).
- Every page ends with one clear next step.
- Citations and internal links are inline markdown on a 1 to 3 word contextual anchor. Never "click here", "this study", "learn more", "our services", or a reference list at the bottom. Same rule for external citations and internal links.

## Money page depth
- Service pages target 1500 words or more, justified by intent and proof, not padding. Other page types run as long as the intent requires and no longer.

## Machine-readable layer (specify in each page brief for the Dev agent)
- JSON-LD: Person (with sameAs profile URLs from `seo/context/author.md`), ProfessionalService, one Service per money page, FAQPage where real Q&A exists, Article with author and dates per post, BreadcrumbList.
- A unique Open Graph image per page, generated by the Dev agent (branded, per page, drawn from the design system), never a placeholder.
- An llms.txt file at the site root.

## Mandatory QA pass (Project Track pre-launch; output a short qa-report)
1. No page violates any standard stated anywhere on the site.
2. No metric lacks a unit and timeframe. No vanity metric sits on a page that disavows them.
3. Claims the site makes about itself (fast, accessible, structured) are true of the delivered code. Flag any the Dev agent must verify.
4. Duplicate-string scan passed for both sentences and labels.
5. Every CTA and internal link resolves to a real planned URL.
6. Each page has one primary keyword, 2 to 3 secondaries, a labelled intent, and a single CTA.

## Per-run QA (Operations Track)
- `content-writer`: the Step 5 self-review checklist must pass before finishing. On a persistent fail, set the queue item to `needs_review`.
- `onsite-audit`: technical, schema, and Core Web Vitals health on the URLs in `seo/context/audit-urls.txt`. Money pages from `seo/context/services.md` get the tighter score thresholds.
- `refresh-recommender`: indexing and staleness actions via the GSC MCP. Cite the `coverage_state` verbatim. Recommend, never auto-rewrite.
