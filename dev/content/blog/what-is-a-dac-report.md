## DAC Reports in Transportation

### What is a DAC Report?

A DAC report trucking document (DAC=Drive-A-Check) is a comprehensive employment screening tool used throughout the commercial transportation industry. Maintained by HireRight, this detailed record provides authorized companies with crucial information about a driver's professional history. Carriers cross-reference it against their own [driver files](/driver-qualification-files/) to confirm a candidate's record holds up.

## Accessing and Managing Your DAC Report

### Understanding Access Rights

The process is straightforward but requires attention to detail. HireRight, the company maintaining these records, provides one free copy annually to all drivers. This right is protected under federal regulations, ensuring that every professional driver can monitor and verify their employment history.

### Request Process

The request process requires proper documentation to protect your privacy and ensure security. You'll need to submit identification documents along with a completed request form to HireRight. The company offers both digital and physical delivery options, allowing you to choose the most convenient method for your situation.

### Review and Verification

When examining your driver DAC report, thoroughness is essential. Start with a comprehensive review of your employment dates and work history. Pay particular attention to any incidents or safety records, as these significantly impact your employability. DAC report trucking companies rely on this information for hiring decisions, making accuracy crucial for your career.

### Common Areas Requiring Attention

- Employment history spanning the past decade
- Safety records and incident reports
- Performance evaluations from previous employers

- Special certifications and endorsements

### Managing Corrections

If you discover inaccuracies in your report, addressing them promptly is crucial. The correction process begins with documenting the error and gathering supporting evidence. DAC report trucking regulations provide specific procedures for disputing incorrect information, and understanding these procedures helps ensure a successful resolution.

### Ongoing Maintenance

Maintaining accurate records requires proactive involvement. Regular monitoring of your report helps identify and address potential issues before they affect your career opportunities. Professional drivers should establish a routine for reviewing their reports, particularly before beginning new job searches or after changing employers.

### Understanding Report Impact
