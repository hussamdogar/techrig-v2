# audience (working ICP for the content engine)

Compressed from `../../shared/icp.md` (2026-06-19). The operational version System 1/2 use to label intent and pick angles. Full detail and segment C/D live in the shared file.

## Funnel
Acquire on **setup + compliance** → retain long-term on **dispatch**. Same customer, three revenue moments.

## Two segments that matter most
**A. New/aspiring motor carrier (acquisition, priority 1).**
Owner-operator going independent. Needs USDOT/MC, BOC-3, UCR, LLC, drug & alcohol consortium, authority activation.
- Pains: FMCSA paperwork maze, fear of filing wrong, authority not activating, no clear order of operations.
- Triggers: bought a truck, leaving a carrier, MC submitted but inactive.
- Searches: "how to start a trucking company," "how to get MC and DOT number," "what is a BOC-3," "UCR registration."
- Intent: informational → transactional. **Greenfield — build here first.**

**B. Active owner-operator (retention via dispatch, current rankings).**
1-3 trucks, one trailer type. Wants more profitable loads, less admin.
- Pains: deadhead, low rates, load-board grind, billing.
- Triggers: cheap-load stretch, outgrowing self-dispatch, bad prior dispatcher.
- Searches: "truck dispatch service," "[trailer] dispatching," "best load boards."
- Intent: commercial → transactional. **Defend and upgrade existing silos.**

## Secondary
- Scaling small fleet (3-20 trucks): compliance upkeep + multi-truck dispatch.
- Brokers/freight forwarders: BOC-3 process agent only; do not dilute carrier-first voice.

## Content rule of thumb
Setup/compliance pages always point forward to dispatch. Dispatch pages assume the reader may still need compliance upkeep. Every page picks ONE segment + funnel stage and writes to it.
