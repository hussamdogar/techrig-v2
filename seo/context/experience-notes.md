## Client setup stories (6 documented, all publishable)
Frame each as a problem → what Tech Rig actually did → outcome. Never imply a guaranteed activation date; the 21-day FMCSA protest period is outside Tech Rig's control.

1. **Art Guiteraz, Affordable Hauling Services LLC (CA, box truck).** Applied himself but registered as a *freight forwarder*, not a motor carrier, so the authority did not permit hauling freight in his own truck. Tech Rig filed a new MC application under the correct motor-carrier classification, plus BOC-3 and UCR, all within 24 hours. Authority activated right after the mandatory 21-day protest period; still active. Lesson hook: wrong authority classification is as damaging as an incomplete filing.

2. **Marcus, MARX LLC (MD, CDL hotshot, Ram 3500 dually).** New owner-operator needing company- and driver-level compliance before hauling. Tech Rig handled BOC-3, UCR, Driver Qualification file, Drug & Alcohol Consortium, Clearinghouse setup, and pre-employment drug-test coordination. Compliance completed in ~7 days; began hauling after the 21-day protest period. (Gave a short testimonial — see below.)

3. **Felix, Diamond in the Rough Towing and Trucking (NJ, CDL power-only).** Experienced driver who self-filed some steps, then needed BOC-3, DQ file, consortium, Clearinghouse guidance, pre-employment drug test, and an MCS-150 address correction (he had omitted his apartment number, and his insurer warned the policy could be cancelled if the FMCSA record was not corrected). Most services done in 24-48 hours, none over 7 days; corrected address reflected within 24 hours; authority activated after the protest period. Lesson hooks: owner-operators still need DQ files; a stale (>30-day-old) drug test required a fresh pre-employment test.

4. **Eduardo (CA, box truck).** Had a USDOT and MC but missed his insurance/BOC-3 deadline, so FMCSA dismissed the MC. He wanted to reinstate it to keep a 1-year-old MC for Amazon Relay eligibility. Tech Rig had to explain a dismissed MC cannot be reinstated: filed a brand-new operating-authority application (he keeps the old MC number, but the age clock resets to 0), set up his FMCSA portal, and filed MC + BOC-3 + UCR within 1 day, then waited out the 21-day protest period. Now fully active, has hired a driver, moving onto ongoing driver compliance. Lesson hook: a dismissed MC resets the authority-age clock that brokers like Amazon care about.

5. **Gustavo (CA, box truck).** Wanted to restart using an old, inactive MC, but FMCSA's migration to the new MOTUS system meant his company records would not surface, blocking reinstatement. Tech Rig caught the migration glitch, opened a support ticket, submitted corporate documents to bridge old data to MOTUS (account created after ~7 days), then hit a Pay.gov payment failure and opened a second ticket. Status: system-migration hurdles cleared, waiting on FMCSA to fix the payment glitch. Lesson hook: MOTUS records often must be claimed/linked/corrected before any filing can proceed.

6. **Tanisha (NC, power-only).** Self-filed USDOT + MC with many errors; came in to buy a BOC-3 but needed broad MC corrections. With no active MOTUS account she was locked out of online edits, and claiming an existing USDOT on MOTUS needs a USDOT PIN that FMCSA no longer emails (weeks-long wait for a mailed letter). Tech Rig first mailed paper MCS-150 and OP-1 forms; after ~1 month with no response, switched to triggering an official FMCSA callback for live-agent identity verification. Status: awaiting callback to clear the MOTUS account, after which BOC-3, UCR, consortium, Clearinghouse, and DQ files execute. Lesson hook: the MOTUS PIN trap, and how a callback breaks the deadlock.

## Operational lessons (proof-rich angles for pages and posts)
- Submitting an MC/USDOT application does not mean a carrier is ready to operate; authority can fail to activate at day 21 if BOC-3, insurance, UCR, or another requirement was not completed correctly.
- Choosing the wrong authority classification (e.g. freight forwarder vs motor carrier) blocks lawful hauling.
- Owner-operators are still drivers for DQ-file purposes; being the only driver does not waive the DQ requirement.
- Drug-testing is time-sensitive; a >30-day-old test requires a fresh pre-employment test.
- Small FMCSA-record errors cause big problems (missing apartment number nearly triggered an insurance cancellation).
- The legacy-to-MOTUS transition adds delays; records must often be claimed/linked/corrected before filings can proceed. Tech Rig's role is diagnosing what is actually blocking a carrier and sequencing the fix, not just submitting forms.
