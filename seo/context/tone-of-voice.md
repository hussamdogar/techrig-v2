# tone-of-voice

Drafted by the SEO build from the live site's current voice + project_config voice notes (2026-06-19). DRAFT — confirm with the business. Governs voice within `_shared/standards.md` (which it cannot relax).

## Who is speaking
A working trucking-operations partner who has filed real authorities and dispatched real loads. Talks to owner-operators and small fleet owners as a peer, not a corporate vendor. Confident, direct, plain-spoken. Knows the regulations cold and explains them without jargon.

## Voice principles
- **Plain and operational.** Short sentences. Concrete trucking specifics (BOC-3, MC#, deadhead, load boards, lanes) over abstractions.
- **Outcome-first.** Lead with what the driver gets: authority activated, trucks loaded, fewer empty miles.
- **Direct, lightly confident.** Strong claims only where proof backs them. No hype, no hard sell.
- **Respect the reader's time and money.** They are running a business on thin margins.

## Current-site voice (observed)
The revamped homepage uses punchy, benefit-led headers ("Your Authority Won't Activate Without BOC-3," "Now Let's Keep Your Trucks Loaded," "Turn Texas Miles Into Texas-Sized Profits"). Keep the directness and energy; cut the hype-listicle register seen in older blog titles.

## Sample sentences (on-voice)
1. "Your MC number is approved, but without a BOC-3 on file your authority will not activate. We file it the same day, in all 50 states."
2. "Once your authority is live, we keep your truck loaded: real loads, negotiated rates, no forced dispatch."
3. "Most new carriers lose their first month to paperwork. We handle the filings so you can start hauling."

## Off-voice (do not write like this)
- Hype listicles: "Revolutionize Your Trucking Business," "Unleashing Profit Potential," "Game-Changing Benefits." (Also violates the banned-words list in standards.)
- Generic filler that could describe any dispatcher with the trailer type swapped out.

## Reading level
FAQ and process sections at Grade 8. Hero sections may be punchier but must pass a 5-second clarity test.
