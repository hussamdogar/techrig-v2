# Keyword Research: DGR Tech Rig

DataForSEO (volume, search intent, CPC, Keyword Difficulty), US, 2026-06-19, combined with first-party GSC ranking queries (Phase 3). Volume = avg monthly searches. KD = DataForSEO Keyword Difficulty 0-100 (blank in source = negligible/unmeasured, shown as ~0-5). Nothing fabricated; "na" where DataForSEO returned no value.

## How to read intent vs funnel stage
DataForSEO labels regulatory terms "informational" or "navigational," but for a done-for-you service these carry real buyer intent: someone searching "boc-3 filing" or "ucr registration" usually wants it handled. The **Funnel Stage** column reflects true buyer intent for this business; the **Intent** column shows DataForSEO's raw label. Business Value (High/Med/Low) weighs commercial relevance + CPC + funnel role. Priority (P1/P2/P3) weighs value × winnability given techrig's low authority (AS 10) — favoring high-volume, low-KD, commercially relevant terms.

## Strategic frame
- **Authority is low (AS 10).** Win where KD is low and intent is commercial. Avoid head terms owned by .gov (fmcsa registration KD 60, dot number KD 38, usdot number KD 29) as primary battles; use them as secondaries on hub pages.
- **Compliance/setup is the prize:** high volume, mostly low-moderate KD, and techrig ranks for none of it today.
- **Box truck dispatch is the franchise to defend/deepen;** other trailer types are low-volume secondaries.

## Head terms = themes, never page targets
"dot number," "usdot number," "fmcsa registration," "trucking authority," "truck dispatcher" are high-volume but gov/marketplace-owned or too broad. Use them as **secondary keywords and H2s on the relevant hub/money page**, not as standalone primary targets.
