Wordmark and loader

Primary identity is a monospace registration wordmark: the language of DOT numbers, VINs, and official records, and the same face the design system already uses for prices, statuses, and filing numbers. The bespoke element is the amber register tick between the words. The loader reuses the "name as a rig" idea (direction D) as a working-state animation only, never as the logo.

Primary lockup

Wordmark plus descriptor. The descriptor "Compliance + Dispatch" is a placeholder; final tagline wording is SEO and brand's call, the design just reserves the slot.

  • Single colour: the tick takes the text colour, so the mark survives one-colour print, fax, and stamping. Amber is an accent that drops in one-colour use.

Loader (working state, from direction D)

The two words become the truck: TECH is the cab, RIG the trailer, coupled by the amber hitch. The rig holds centre while the road scrolls beneath it, so it reads as driving. It bobs slightly for life. This is a loading device, not the logo. Reduced-motion users get the static rig with no movement.

Do not

  • Do not set the wordmark in any face other than IBM Plex Mono, or in lower case.
  • Do not colour the wordmark amber, add a gradient, glow, outline, or container box around it.
  • Do not use the rig loader as the logo, on letterhead, or as the favicon.
  • Do not stretch, condense, or re-space the letters; the tracking is part of the mark.
