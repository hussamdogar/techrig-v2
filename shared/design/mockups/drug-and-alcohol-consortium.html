What a drug and alcohol consortium is

A consortium/third-party administrator (C/TPA) is an organization that manages DOT drug and alcohol testing for carriers, including the random testing pool. FMCSA requires CDL drivers to be part of a testing program, and an owner-operator cannot run their own random pool of one, so they join a consortium. The program covers pre-employment, random, post-accident, and other required testing, and ties into the FMCSA Clearinghouse.

Why it works this way An owner-operator cannot run their own random pool of one, so they join a consortium.

Who needs to be in a consortium

  • Owner-operators with a CDL operating under FMCSA.
  • Carriers with CDL drivers (the carrier runs the program, the consortium administers it).

Note: not every operation is subject to CDL drug-and-alcohol rules. Whether this applies depends on your vehicles, drivers, and operation. We confirm before you enroll.

Pre-employment drug test

Before a driver can perform safety-sensitive work, they generally need a negative pre-employment drug test on file. Timing matters: a test that is too old does not count. We coordinate pre-employment testing so the timing is right and the result is documented in the driver's qualification file.

Worked example · Past result For a New Jersey power-only carrier, an earlier drug test was more than 30 days old, so a new pre-employment test had to be arranged before the driver could meet the requirement.

Consortium and the Clearinghouse

The FMCSA Clearinghouse is the federal database of drug and alcohol violations. Your consortium program and Clearinghouse registration work together: queries and reporting flow between them. New carriers usually need both set up before hauling, which is why we handle them as a driver-compliance set with your DQ files.

Drug and alcohol consortium FAQ

Do owner-operators need a consortium?
If you hold a CDL and operate under FMCSA, yes. You cannot run a random pool of one, so you join a consortium.
What testing does the program cover?
Pre-employment, random, post-accident, and other DOT-required testing.
Does every carrier need this?
It depends on your vehicles, drivers, and operation. We confirm whether CDL drug-and-alcohol rules apply to you before you enroll.
How much does it cost?
Consortium enrollment is $150; a pre-employment drug test is $100.
Is this connected to the Clearinghouse?
Yes. Your consortium program and Clearinghouse registration work together. We can set up both.
