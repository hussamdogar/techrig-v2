What IFTA registration is

The International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) simplifies fuel-tax reporting for carriers operating in more than one state or province.

  • You register in your base jurisdiction.
  • You receive an IFTA license and decals (stickers) for your vehicles.
  • You file one quarterly fuel-tax return that reconciles the fuel you bought against the miles you ran in each state.

Applies to you

  • Qualifying interstate vehicles, generally those over a weight threshold or with enough axles.
  • Carriers operating in more than one state or province.

Does not apply the same way

  • If you run purely intrastate, it may not apply.

IFTA vs IRP

IFTA is about fuel tax.

IRP is about registration and apportioned plates.

Most interstate carriers need both, and we set them up together, but they are separate programs.

IFTA registration FAQ

What is an IFTA sticker?
A decal placed on your vehicle showing it is registered under the International Fuel Tax Agreement. You get them with your IFTA license.
How often do I file IFTA?
Quarterly. You report miles and fuel by state and settle the difference. Keeping mileage and fuel records as you go makes this simple.
Do I need IFTA and IRP?
Most interstate carriers need both. IFTA is fuel tax; IRP is plates and registration.
What if I run in only one state?
IFTA is for interstate operation. If you run purely intrastate, it may not apply. We confirm before you pay.
