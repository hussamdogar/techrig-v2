# ICP: DGR Tech Rig

Cross-workspace contract (SEO → Design, Dev). Defined 2026-06-19 from the live crawl + US owner-operator market knowledge. The site had no documented ICP; this is built around the confirmed funnel: **acquire on setup + compliance, retain long-term on dispatch.**

## The funnel in one line
A driver becomes an independent carrier (we file their authority and compliance), gets on the road (we keep them loaded via dispatch), and stays for years (recurring retainer). The same person moves through all three of our revenue moments. Content must catch them at the start (setup/compliance) and hold them at scale (dispatch).

## Primary segment A — The new/aspiring motor carrier  (ACQUISITION, highest priority)
The owner-operator going independent: getting their own operating authority instead of driving under someone else's.
- **Who:** Experienced company driver or lease-operator buying their first truck and starting an LLC; 1 truck, often the driver themselves. US-based, all 50 states.
- **Jobs to be done:** Get USDOT + MC number, file BOC-3, register UCR, set up the LLC, enroll in the drug & alcohol consortium, get insurance in place, and actually *activate* authority without weeks of delay.
- **Pains:** The FMCSA paperwork maze; fear of filing something wrong and getting rejected or fined; not knowing the order of operations; authority sitting inactive because a BOC-3 was never filed; conflicting advice from forums and YouTube.
- **Buying triggers:** Just bought/financed a truck; leaving a carrier to go solo; MC application submitted but authority not active; got a compliance notice; "starting a trucking company this month."
- **Objections:** "Can't I just do this myself on the FMCSA site?"; "Is this a scam / will my authority actually activate?"; "How much are government fees vs. your fee?"; "How long will it take?"
- **Search language:** "how to start a trucking company," "how to get MC and DOT number," "what is a BOC-3," "do I need a BOC-3," "how to get authority for trucking," "UCR registration," "new authority not active."
- **Funnel stage / intent:** TOFU → BOFU. Informational (how-to) shading into transactional (file my BOC-3, start my company). This is where SEO must win first, because it has no pages here today.

## Primary segment B — The active owner-operator  (RETENTION via dispatch, current revenue base)
Has authority, is hauling, wants more money and less admin. This is who the existing silos already rank for.
- **Who:** 1-3 truck owner-operator, one trailer type (reefer, flatbed, dry van, box truck, power only, hot shot). Running 1-5 years.
- **Jobs to be done:** Keep the truck loaded with profitable freight, cut deadhead, negotiate better rates, offload load-board grind and billing/paperwork.
- **Pains:** Empty miles, low rates, hours lost on load boards, late invoices/factoring hassle, burnout doing dispatch themselves.
- **Buying triggers:** A bad stretch of cheap loads; a missed-load week; growing past self-dispatch; a bad experience with a previous dispatcher.
- **Objections:** "Why pay a % when I can use the load board myself?"; "Will I be forced into loads I don't want?"; "Am I locked into a contract?" (We answer: no forced dispatch, no long-term contract.)
- **Search language:** "truck dispatch service," "[trailer] dispatching," "best load boards for [trailer]," "dispatcher fees," "how to find a dispatcher for a box truck."
- **Funnel stage / intent:** MOFU → BOFU. Commercial investigation + transactional.

## Secondary segment C — The scaling small fleet (3-20 trucks)
- **Who:** Owner running a small fleet, no full back office.
- **Jobs:** Ongoing compliance upkeep (biennial updates, clearinghouse, UCR tier as truck count rises, MCS-150) + dispatch capacity for multiple trucks.
- **Triggers:** Adding trucks/drivers; biennial update due; UCR tier change; a CSA/audit scare.
- **Intent:** Commercial + navigational; values reliability and "handle it for me."

## Tertiary segment D — Brokers / freight forwarders (process-agent only)
- The homepage names brokers and freight forwarders; they need a BOC-3 process agent too. Low strategic weight but easy capture from BOC-3 content. Do not let it dilute the carrier-first positioning.

## What this means for the build
- **Lead with segment A.** Build the compliance/setup money pages and the how-to-start-a-trucking-company content cluster first; it is greenfield and it is the top of the funnel that feeds dispatch.
- **Defend segment B.** Keep and upgrade the dispatch silos that already rank; tie them to A with internal links ("authority active? now keep it loaded").
- **The bridge is the story:** every setup page should point forward to dispatch; every dispatch page should assume the reader may still need compliance upkeep.

## Confirmed with the business (2026-06-19)
- **Brand-new, single-truck carriers ARE a wanted client type.** Segment A is a real, prioritized target, not an assumption.
- **Consultancy targets the same segments** (A/C), not a distinct one.
