### H1: How to Start a Trucking Company
**Hero lede:**
Starting a trucking company is a sequence of decisions and federal filings, and the order matters. Do it in the right order and you are hauling in weeks. Do it out of order and you can finish all the paperwork only to find your authority will not activate. This guide walks the whole path, from forming your company to passing your first safety audit, and points you to the exact filing when you are ready to act. It is written from the filings we run every week, including the ones that go wrong.

**Primary CTA (soft, mid and end):** "Want this done for you? See our [compliance services](/compliance-services/)." Pillar CTA is a soft route, not a hard sell.

### H2: Before you file: decisions that shape everything
- **What will you haul, and where?** Cargo type and interstate vs intrastate decide which authority and filings you need.
- **What equipment?** Box truck, hotshot, reefer, flatbed, dry van, or power only. This affects insurance and, later, how you get loads.
- **Carrier or broker?** They are different authorities with different requirements.
A wrong call here is the single most expensive mistake we fix. One carrier registered as a freight forwarder when he meant to haul his own freight, which did not authorize him to operate, and it cost him a refile and weeks of delay.

### H2: How to start a trucking company in 9 steps
Numbered, Grade 8, each step linking to its money page:
1. **Form your business entity.** Most carriers set up an [LLC](/trucking-llc/) and get an EIN. This separates your business and personal liability.
2. **Get your USDOT number.** Your federal ID as a carrier. See [USDOT registration](/dot-registration/).
3. **Get your MC operating authority** if you will haul regulated freight for hire. See [MC authority](/mc-registration/).
4. **File your BOC-3.** Names a process agent in every state; required before authority activates. See [BOC-3 filing](/boc-3-filing/).
5. **Get insurance and have it filed with FMCSA.** Your authority will not activate until the insurance filing is in. See [insurance filing](/trucking-insurance-filing/).
6. **Register for UCR.** Annual, based on fleet size. See [UCR registration](/ucr-registration/).
7. **Set up driver compliance:** [driver qualification files](/driver-qualification-files/), [Clearinghouse](/fmcsa-clearinghouse-registration/), and a [drug and alcohol consortium](/drug-and-alcohol-consortium/) with pre-employment testing. This applies even if you are the only driver.
8. **Handle interstate operations filings:** [IRP apportioned plates](/irp-registration/) and [IFTA](/ifta-registration/) if you run across state lines, plus an [ELD](/eld-services/).
9. **Get through the 21-day protest period and your first safety audit.** Authority can activate after the mandatory protest period; new carriers face a safety audit early, so your driver files and records need to be in order.

### H2: The mistakes that stall new carriers
Proof-rich, from real work (no names needed here, drawn from `experience-notes.md` lessons):
- **Submitting an application is not the same as being ready to operate.** Carriers reach day 21 and cannot activate because the BOC-3, insurance, or UCR was missing or wrong.
- **The wrong authority classification** blocks the work you intended to do.
- **Owner-operators still need driver qualification files.** Being the only driver does not waive it.
- **Drug tests expire.** A pre-employment test older than 30 days can mean a new one before a driver can start.
- **Small record errors cause big problems.** A missing apartment number on an FMCSA record nearly got a carrier's insurance cancelled.
- **The MOTUS transition adds delays.** Records often must be claimed or linked before a filing will go through.

### H2: How to start a trucking company FAQ
FAQPage schema, Grade 8:
- "How long does it take to start a trucking company?" The filings can be done quickly, but FMCSA requires a mandatory 21-day protest period before authority activates, and processing time varies. We do not promise a date.
- "Do I need an LLC to start a trucking company?" Not legally required, but most carriers form one to separate business and personal liability and to get an EIN.
- "Do I need both a USDOT and an MC number?" Many interstate for-hire carriers need both. Some carriers need only a USDOT number. It depends on your operation.
- "Can I do this myself?" Yes. Most of it is doable. People come to us when classifications, MOTUS, or the order of filings trips them up, or when they would rather not risk a delay.
- "What is the first thing I should do?" Decide what and where you will haul and your equipment, then form your entity. Those choices drive every filing after.
